# answerable-software
*On software systems that localize what they make impossible, what they commit to, and where their consequences return.*

---

Most software architectures are evaluated by what they produce.
This corpus evaluates them by what they can answer for; by whether their structure makes their guarantees real,
their commitments visible, and their failures correctable in the right place.

Answerability is treated here as a structural property of the system, not a behavior of the team that maintains it.

---

## What this corpus is

A working library of essays, diagnostics, and operational tools on the structural conditions under which software systems become answerable for what they do.

Each folder treats one structural question: how a boundary institutes a core, what makes a model substantive, when a test proves a consequence rather than a path, where a domain hides when it has no legitimate place to live.

The essays are written to stand alone. Together they form a single argument: that the quality of a software system is measured not by the cleanness of its form, but by its capacity to localize what it engages.

## What this corpus is not

It is not a methodology. There is no *answerable-driven development* to adopt, no ceremony to perform, no certification to acquire.

It is not a critique of existing approaches. Hexagonal architecture, domain-driven design, clean architecture, test-driven development, etc.. These are read here as partial answers to a question they did not fully name. The corpus extends them; it does not replace them.

It is not a collection of patterns. Patterns describe recurring solutions; this corpus describes recurring conditions under which solutions become real or remain decorative.
